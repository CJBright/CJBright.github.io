The University of Manchester, UK 		Jan 2020 - Present
Expected end October 2023
  My thesis applies computational fluid dynamics (CFD) to cardiovascular surgery as a pre-surgical planning tool. The work aims to determine the optimum shape and position of a coronary artery bypass graft, providing clinical guidance where there currently is none. I worked closely with cardiovascular surgeons to ensure my studies balanced the practicalities of clinical implementation, as well as the limited computational resources of the UK's National Health Service. I have presented my work as posters and oral presentations at conferences including the European Society for Artificial Organs, European Society for Biomechanics, and the World Council of Biomechanics.
