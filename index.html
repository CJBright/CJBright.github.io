Bio

Hi, I'm Charlie!
I'm an academic researcher in computational fluid dynamics at the University of Manchester. My PhD applies traditional engineering techniques to problems faced in cardiovascular surgery, bridging the gap between biology and engineering. I use an open-source pipeline for the development of my studies, which has introduced a variety of development tools to me which I have enjoyed learning. As the PhD draws to a close, I'm looking for further opportunities to merge my mechanical engineering knowledge with software engineering practices. Recent work has proven my worth as a capable independent worker; however, I am looking for a stimulating environment that encourages teamwork and supports development opportunities. I am a socially orientated character both away-from and behind the screen. I enjoy catch-ups over a coffee, beer, or a couple of games. I'm particularly fond of team-strategy games like 'DotA 2' where communication and efficiency are paramount for success. My love of DotA has forged friendships the world over, initiated a love for travel, and even taught me languages! Today I speak Swedish, which I began learning after travelling to gaming competition in Jönköping, Sweden and staying with a friend and their family. The experience opened my eyes to the value of learning languages and embracing the culture of different countries.
